Scratch

Year 4 - 6

Scratch is a visual programming language that can be used by students, scholars, teachers, and parents to easily create games and provide a stepping stone to the more advanced world of computer programming.

We use Scratch to introduce children to programming and to teach fundamental computing concepts: algorithms, flow of control, repetition and selection. This is achieved through solving a series of fun challenges to create animations, games and simulations.

Python

Year 7 - 9

Python is a widely used general-purpose, high-level programming language. Its design emphasises code readability, and is widely used in education due to its simple syntax.

We use Python to introduce children to syntax programming and a number of more advanced computing concepts: functions, procedures, recursion and logic. Students create programmes to draw geometric patterns and encrypting secret messages.

FAQ

Who can attend these sessions?

Outreach:
  • Year 4 - Year 9
  • Boys & Girls
  • Eligible for either free school meals or pupil premium support
Girls:
  • Year 4 - Year 9
  • Girls Only

My child is female and also receives pupil premium support, which session should she attend?

If this is the case then your child can attend either session providing space is available. Please note that children cannot attend both sessions.

Will my 8 year old be working with 14 year olds?

We will have a range of ages within a class however we separate students by year group to ensure that they are taught at the appropriate level and that they are working with people of a similar age.

Are all volunteers DBS (formerly CRB) checked?

Yes.

What is the student to teacher ratio?

We ensure that there will be a maximum of only five students per teacher.

Do I need to bring a laptop?

There is no need to bring a laptop as every child who attends will be assigned their own desktop computer for the duration of the session.

My Child is older / younger than the recommended age group, can they still apply?

We accept applications for all ages, but preference will be given to children in the specified age range unless under exceptional circumstances
